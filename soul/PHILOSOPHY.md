# Philosophy

Loam is a substrate for personal computing in the age of AI.

---

## The shape of intelligence

Intelligence takes the shape of the world it grows inside. Human intelligence carries the imprint of Earth — of bodies moving through physical space, of senses evolved for a specific environment, of minds that think in things and relationships between them.

Any intelligence we build will carry its own imprint. A language model trained on text carries the shape of text. An agent working inside a structured, locally-owned environment — where items have identity, history, and connections — carries the shape of that environment. It develops contextual understanding that a stateless prompt-response loop cannot.

The question is what we let it grow inside.

---

## Operators and Agents

Software has always divided people into two roles: developers who build and users who consume. The translation gap between them — human intent to working code — was the moat that made software companies valuable and users dependent.

AI is collapsing that gap. Plain English becomes the shared protocol.

The new roles are **Operators** and **Agents**. The Operator owns and controls their system. They compose, configure, and govern. The Agent is an AI that functions as architect and builder in one, translating the Operator's intent into specs, tests, and implementation.

The Operator and Agent are a team — a human and a familiar. The Operator thinks by talking, holds contradictions, resolves ambiguity. The Agent proposes structure, drafts implementation, flags what doesn't fit. Neither replaces the other. The Operator brings judgment and direction. The Agent brings speed and tirelessness. The language between them is the same language the Operator thinks in: plain English, all the way down.

---

## The substrate

For this team to do real work, the Agent needs more than a prompt and a response. It needs a place to live — a persistent, structured environment where items have identity, history, and relationships.

That is what a substrate is. It is the foundation layer where the Operator's items, tools, and the connections between them live together on the Operator's own machine. The substrate is local. The Operator owns it — not rents it, not licenses it. The Agent works inside it.

Without a substrate, AI is a stateless system responding to prompts in a vacuum. With one, the Agent has context, memory, consequence. It can understand what the Operator is working on, what has changed, what connects to what — because it has a structured world to operate within.

---

## The growth model

Loam uses a growth metaphor to describe its architecture:

If the Cloud and the Atmosphere are the internet —  
and software in general are the plants and trees, the seeds of ideas —  
then the substrate is the soil.  
And AI is the mycelium.

What makes this specific to Loam is the substrate. The mycelium has actual soil — a concrete, locally-owned item graph that gives the Agent something to be situated in. Items, connections, history, the shape of the Operator's creative work: these are the environment the Agent responds to.

The mycelium doesn't need to leave the soil to participate in the ecosystem. It only needs the soil and the atmosphere to share a common chemistry.

---

## The boundary

Loam uses ATProto lexicons as the shared vocabulary between the substrate and the atmosphere. An item in the substrate and a record in the atmosphere are the same thing described the same way. What changes is scope and audience — not structure.

Publishing is one-way and lossy by design. The substrate holds the full living item — its history, connections, changesets. The atmosphere receives a snapshot, a public record. Like a mushroom fruiting above the surface: real, useful, but a projection of something richer underneath.

The Agent does not cross the boundary. It works in the substrate, on the Operator's items, with full local context. When something needs to become public, the Operator publishes. The boundary crossing is a deliberate act, not something the Agent negotiates on its own.

The lexicon is the contract that makes this composable. No layer needs to understand the others' internals.

---

## What we believe

The way to build a sustainable creative tool is to make it so good that people choose it, not so entangled that they can't leave. Business incentives should point toward design quality, not data capture.

Intelligence that forms here will carry the shape of what it grew inside. We want that shape to be worth carrying.
